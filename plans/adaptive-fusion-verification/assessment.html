Key Findings

  1. The highest-return seam already exists: builder feedback, resume/replacement, and convergence policy are implemented and tested, but the authoritative orchestrator still fails and skips.
  2. The current verification contract is narrower than its name: it governs declared browser/persona evidence, not pre-build behavioral requirements or revision authority.
  3. Review provenance stops before synthesis: sources survive, but consensus, unique value, conflicts, retained decisions, discarded proposals, and rationale do not.
  4. Routing lacks leverage signals: kind, complexity, and risk exist in different places, but uncertainty, consequence/reversibility, and selective-synthesis tier are absent.
  5. Metrics cannot price incremental quality: global totals cannot show whether a second model contributed a retained unique finding or changed a decision.
  6. The checked-out branch contains a stale review contract: it still says per-chunk dm-review-loop is mandatory, but refreshed origin/main already resolves this in favor of focused Codex review for ordinary chunks, full review for sensitive chunks, and final full dm-review.
  7. Baseline release gate is currently red: 734 tests ran with one error because a live assembly-baseplate/tests/ux declaration no longer satisfies the kernel parser. Six focused kernel suites pass 160 tests, and routing/economics validation passes. Planning must isolate or deliberately repair this pre-existing cross-repository fixture coupling.

Recommendations

  1. Promote bounded builder feedback into the authoritative orchestrator first, while keeping kernel observation and safety boundaries explicit.
  2. Add a versioned behavioral contract artifact after approved planning and before dispatch. Protect its digest and revision authority, not every test file.
  3. Extend existing dm-review convergence rather than adding a generic fusion command.
  4. Add uncertainty and consequence to approved plan/manifest data and derive a fast, verified, or synthesized workflow tier.
  5. Emit per-attempt provider/model/token/time/cost/finding receipts and calculate retained incremental yield.
  6. Preserve the already-resolved review tiers from origin/main when adding convergence behavior; do not reintroduce per-chunk full-review overhead.
  7. Keep local/sovereign inference and multi-round debate out of this scope.
