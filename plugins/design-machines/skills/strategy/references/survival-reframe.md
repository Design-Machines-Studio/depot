# The Survival Reframe

Worker co-ops outlast conventional businesses. This is the single most underused fact in pro-co-op messaging. It also inverts the frame every software pitch to co-ops takes.

## The numbers

| Region | Worker co-op survival | Conventional business survival | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| United Kingdom | 76% at 5 years | ~42% at 5 years (all new companies) | Co-operatives UK, drawing on UK Office for National Statistics business demography data |
| France | 80–90% at 3 years (worker co-ops, SCOP) | ~66% at 3 years (conventional) | CG Scop / Review of Industrial Organisation 2015 |
| Italy | 87% at 3 years (worker-buyout co-ops) | 48% at 3 years (all Italian businesses) | CECOP / CICOPA research |

(Full citations and verified URLs live at `plugins/design-machines/skills/audience/references/survival-reframe-citations.md`.)

## The 2008 stress test

During the global financial crisis, worker-owned employment in France *grew* 4.2% while non-co-op employment shrank 0.7%. CICOPA's "Resilience of the cooperative model" report attributes this to indivisible reserves and longer-horizon decision-making. Worker co-ops are not a fragile organizational form. They are a more durable one.

## Why this reframes the pitch

The standard sales motion to co-ops assumes fragility. *Build resilience. Don't fail. Avoid the 5-year curse.* Software pitches lean into rescue language. The data says the opposite. Co-ops already outlast the businesses pitching them software.

Assembly is positioned as *amplifying an existing competitive advantage*, not life support for a fragile form. That single shift in framing changes the register of every conversation.

This belongs in:

- Assembly's homepage hero
- Propaganda for the movement
- Conference talks
- Federation outreach
- Member-facing onboarding (the survival numbers are confidence-building for new members nervous about whether the form is "real")
- Every Design Machines artifact that touches the politics of work

## Where to use this

- **Homepage hero:** "You already last longer. Assembly is how you compound that lead."
- **Federation outreach:** lead with the durability fact, not the feature list. Federation staff live with the exhausting stereotype that they manage decline. The data lets them lead with strength.
- **Conference talks:** open with the numbers; let the audience do the math on what that says about who's selling who.
- **Member-facing onboarding:** the survival numbers calm new members nervous about whether the form is "real."
- **Direct co-op sales calls:** lands quietly inside a discovery call. "You're already running a more durable business form than most of the companies that pitch you software. Assembly compounds that advantage."

## Why this lands inside DM voice

Materialist. Empirically grounded. Refuses the rescue framing the SaaS world defaults to. The numbers do the work. No swagger needed.
