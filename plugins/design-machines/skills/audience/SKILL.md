# DM Audience Research

Canonical home for Design Machines' April 22, 2026 audience research. Use this skill whenever the question touches who Design Machines is talking to, what to say to them, or how to position against the alternatives.

The research lives in `references/full-research.md`. Use the extracted references for specific surfaces. Reach for the full doc when you need depth.

## Primary audience

Co-op developers, federations, and incubators. USFWC, CWCF, DAWI (Democracy at Work Institute), Cooperation Works!, Cooperatives Europe orbit. They recommend Assembly to dozens of member co-ops each. Channel-first GTM.

## Secondary audience

Existing small worker co-ops directly. The Tuesday-morning member who needs governance to feel real, not be homework.

## Tertiary audience

The broader labor and solidarity-economy public. Door-opening content via the survival reframe and workplace-democracy framing.

## The six findings (one-line version)

1. **Survival reframe** — co-ops outlast conventional businesses; pitch from strength, not rescue.
2. **Two moats** — integration across fixtures + bylaws become operational.
3. **Sectoral density, not scale** — scale out (replicate), not scale up (grow huge).
4. **Channel-first GTM** — co-op developers, federations, and incubators are primary; existing co-ops are secondary.
5. **Admin debt** — governance equivalent of technical debt; the problem Assembly solves daily.
6. **Decolonizing governance language** — plain-language defaults; bylaws stay legal, members read English.

## Audience vs strategy boundary

This skill is for external comms: pitch material, what to say to specific people, competitive landscape, language discipline, phrases that land, phrases that mark you as a tourist.

The `design-machines:strategy` skill is for internal business decisions: pricing tiers, partnership terms, catalog architecture, financial planning, runway, the Live Wires conversion funnel.

If the question is about *what to say to someone*, this skill. If the question is about *what to decide internally*, strategy.
