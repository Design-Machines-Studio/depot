# Survival Reframe — Citations

Verified citations for the three survival statistics used in `survival-reframe.md` and across DM external communication. Verify URLs are live before quoting in published material — citations rot.

Verification was performed via WebSearch on 2026-04-22 with multiple query variations per stat. Status notes below record what was found.

## United Kingdom — 76% co-op five-year survival vs ~42% all new companies

- **Source:** Co-operatives UK, *Co-operative Business Survival* research report (drawing on UK Office for National Statistics business demography data).
- **Statistic as stated:** "76% of co-operatives are still in business at five years, compared to ~42% of all new companies."
- **Primary URL (PDF):** https://www.uk.coop/sites/default/files/2020-10/co-operative_survival_1.pdf
- **Landing page:** https://www.uk.coop/resources/co-operative-business-survival
- **Status:** Verified live 2026-04-22 via WebSearch.
- **Notes:** Co-operatives UK has published several updates of this analysis. The 2018 *Co-operative Economy* report cites 80% co-op vs 44% conventional at five years. The 2025 *Co-operative and Mutual Economy* analysis cites 82% co-op vs 39% conventional. The 76% / 42% pair is the most widely circulated figure and the version DM has standardized on. Footnote any version drift if the audience is academic.
- **Searched queries:** `Co-operatives UK 76% co-op five year survival rate report` · `Co-operative Business Survival Co-operatives UK PDF` · `UK co-op survival rate five years 76 42`

## France — 80–90% three-year worker co-op survival vs ~66% conventional

- **Source:** CG Scop (Confédération Générale des Sociétés Coopératives) and Review of Industrial Organisation, 2015.
- **Statistic as stated:** "Three-year survival rates for worker cooperatives (SCOP) in France range from 80% to 90%, compared with approximately 66% for conventional businesses."
- **Primary URLs:**
  - CG Scop landing: https://www.les-scop.coop/
  - CICOPA summary citing the 80–90% / 66% framing: https://www.cicopa.coop/work-together-archive/3000-jobs-created-in-worker-cooperatives-in-france/
  - Worker cooperative Wikipedia overview consolidating the figure: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worker_cooperative
- **Status:** Verified live 2026-04-22 via WebSearch. The 80–90% range is found across multiple CG Scop / CICOPA / academic sources; some report a tighter 74–77% three-year figure depending on the year sampled. Use the 80–90% range when citing the most-quoted version; cite the year if the audience is academic.
- **Searched queries:** `CG Scop France worker cooperative survival rate three year` · `SCOP France three-year survival worker cooperative` · `Review of Industrial Organisation 2015 worker cooperative France survival`

## Italy — 87% three-year worker-buyout co-op survival vs 48% all Italian businesses

- **Source:** CECOP / CICOPA (European Confederation of Industrial and Service Cooperatives).
- **Statistic as stated:** "Italian worker-buyout cooperatives show an 87% three-year survival rate, compared with 48% for all Italian businesses (2007–2013 cohort)."
- **Primary URL:** https://www.cicopa.coop/work-together-archive/workers-buy-out-30-years-creating-wealth-in-italy/
- **Supporting:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worker_cooperative (consolidates the figure with citation back to CECOP)
- **Status:** Verified live 2026-04-22 via WebSearch. The exact figure is 87.16% at 3 years for Italian industrial cooperatives born after 2007 and tracked through 2013, the majority being worker buyouts supported by CFI (Cooperazione Finanza Impresa).
- **Notes:** This figure is specific to *worker-buyout* co-ops, not all Italian co-ops. Be precise when citing — saying "Italian co-ops" instead of "Italian worker-buyout co-ops" misstates the population.
- **Searched queries:** `CECOP CICOPA worker buyout cooperative Italy 87% survival rate` · `Italian worker buyout co-op three-year survival 87% CFI` · `CICOPA workers buy-out Italy CFI survival rate`

## 2008 stress test — France worker-owned employment grew 4.2%, non-co-op shrank 0.7%

- **Source:** CICOPA and CG Scop. Most often cited via CICOPA's *The resilience of the cooperative model* (2012) and consolidated on Wikipedia's worker cooperative page.
- **Statistic as stated:** "During the 2008 financial crisis, worker-owned employment in France grew 4.2% while non-co-op employment shrank 0.7%."
- **Primary URLs:**
  - CICOPA *Cooperatives and Employment: a Global Report* (2014): https://www.cicopa.coop/wp-content/uploads/2018/03/cooperatives_and_employment_a_global_report_en__web_21-10_1pag.pdf
  - CICOPA *The resilience of the cooperative model* (2012, Issuu mirror): https://issuu.com/cicopa/docs/report_cecop_2012_en_web
  - Worker cooperative Wikipedia overview consolidating the figure: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worker_cooperative
- **Status:** Verified live 2026-04-22 via WebSearch. The 4.2% growth / 0.7% decline figures appear consistently across CICOPA reports and Wikipedia's worker cooperative page. The CICOPA *Resilience* report attributes the durability to indivisible reserves and longer-horizon decision-making.
- **Searched queries:** `2008 financial crisis France worker-owned employment growth 4.2%` · `CICOPA worker cooperatives France employment 2008 2009 crisis growth resilience` · `"worker cooperatives" "2008" France employment grew "4.2%"`

## Use guidance

- Always cite both the figure AND the source organization in published material.
- Where a URL is paywalled or PDF-only, note that plainly. ("Co-operatives UK 2020 PDF, free download from uk.coop.")
- For talks and social posts where the citation is verbal, lead with the source name (Co-operatives UK; CG Scop; CECOP / CICOPA) — these are recognizable in the audience.
- Update this file when better URLs surface or when newer Co-operatives UK economy reports change the headline figure.
- For academic audiences, include the year of the cohort being cited (2020 Co-operatives UK report; 2007–2013 Italian cohort; 2015 Review of Industrial Organisation paper). The figures vary by methodology and period.
