# Language Reference Card

Curated from the April 22, 2026 audience research. Use freely / use carefully / avoid sets, plus phrases that already land. The terms in **Use freely** are native to the cooperative-developer and federation orbit. **Avoid** terms get external co-op audiences to disengage immediately.

## Use freely

worker-owner, member, patronage, internal capital accounts, AGM, bylaws, the movement, the sector, co-op developer, technical assistance, democratic workplace, workplace democracy, consent decision, block, stand aside, sociocracy, one member one vote, seven cooperative principles, mutual aid, solidarity economy, just transition, the commons, sectoral density, scale out (not scale up), admin debt, operationalizing the bylaws, capacity building, dual power.

## Use carefully

ecosystem (only in context of partner stack), engagement (only in the participation sense, never the metric sense), platform (okay for Assembly, dangerous if it sounds Big Tech), stakeholder (European-friendly, US-ambivalent), empowerment (overused).

## Avoid

users, customers, SaaS, scale the platform, scale up (as a virtue), go-to-market (externally), drive adoption, unlock value, best-in-class, cutting-edge, thought leader, enterprise-grade, engagement (metric sense), solopreneur, ICP, persona (in the marketing sense), TAM, CAC, MRR, churn, runway (externally), pivot, disrupt, synergy, bandwidth (as a metaphor for capacity), ping (as a verb), deck (externally), compliance-as-code (too SaaS-brained — use *operationalizing the bylaws* instead).

## Phrases that already land

- governance is a rhythm, not homework
- inhabited interfaces, not archived snapshots
- governance is infrastructure
- solidarity is the business model
- built for the movement, in the movement
- bylaws become operational, not aspirational
- the rules run the system

## Why the Avoid list matters more than the Use Freely list

A misstep is louder than a hit. One "stakeholder" or "drive adoption" in a federation conversation marks you as a SaaS tourist faster than ten right words can mark you as movement-fluent. The Avoid list is the discipline; the Use Freely list is the upside.
