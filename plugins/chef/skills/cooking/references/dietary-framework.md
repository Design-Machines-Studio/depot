# Eve Persak's Dietary Framework

Consolidated dietary guidelines from nutritionist Eve Persak, personalized for Travis (cardiovascular disease prevention, cholesterol management) and Lydia (hypoglycemia management, glucose spike prevention). Eve's approach: targeted, personalized interventions over generic advice. Cardiovascular, gut, skin, and periodontal health are all connected.

---

## Meal Timing and Pattern

### Morning (within 1-2 hours of waking, around 6:30-7am)

- Small, savory, high-protein first meal. NOT sweet (no smoothie bowls, granola, sweetened things).
- Goal: signal to the body that food is coming, kickstart metabolism.
- Examples: 2 eggs any style, half avocado with olive oil drizzle, half cup Greek yogurt with small amount of berries.
- Keep portions small. Reducing dinner size should create morning hunger (which is good).
- Coffee: black or with unsweetened non-dairy milk (soy, cashew, almond). NOT oat, rice, or coconut milk.

### Midday (around 12pm) -- Best Time for Carbohydrates

- Insulin response is strongest, muscles most receptive to carb input.
- Include fiber-rich whole carbs: brown/red/black rice, legumes, sweet potato, whole grain bread.
- Protein + vegetables + moderate whole carbs.
- Keep portions moderate overall.

### Mid-Afternoon Snack

- 30-40g nuts/seeds to stabilize blood sugar through the afternoon.
- Stay very hydrated (blood pressure support for Lydia).

### Dinner (6-7pm) -- Minimize Carbs

- Reduce overall portion size by 15-20% from current baseline.
- Minimize: noodles, bread, rice, potatoes, sweets, sweetened beverages.
- If carbs included, keep portions very small.
- Focus: protein + generous vegetables + healthy fats/sauces.
- Smaller dinner leads to better digestion, better sleep quality, more morning hunger.
- Consider a 10-15 min easy walk after dinner (not intense, just a stroll for muscle nutrient uptake).

### After Dinner

- Avoid desserts most nights. Special occasions only (target 1x/week).

## Protein Hierarchy (Best to Limit)

### 1. Seafood -- Best Choice Overall

**White fish:** lean, high-quality protein (cod, halibut, sole).

**Cold-water oily fish:** salmon, mackerel, trout, tuna, herring, sardines, anchovies (omega-3s).

**Shellfish:** prawns, scallops, oysters, mussels. These are FINE -- they actually improve lipid profiles.

Wild > farmed, sustainably sourced preferred.

### 2. Eggs -- Whole Eggs Are OK

- Whole eggs do not raise cholesterol the way saturated fats do.
- Egg whites are the "gold standard" protein, saturated-fat-free.
- Technique: 1 whole egg + 2 whites for omelets to cut sat fat while keeping flavor.
- Free range, organic preferred.

### 3. Poultry -- Good Protein

- Lean cuts: breast, white meat.
- Remove skin for regular meals (save skin-on as occasional indulgence).
- Chicken/turkey > duck (leaner).
- Free range, organic preferred.

### 4. Legumes -- Excellent Plant Protein

- High in soluble fiber (lowers cholesterol).
- Lentils (least gas-producing), beans, tempeh, edamame.
- Pre-soaked, well-cooked for digestibility.
- Hummus and legume pastas count.

### 5. Red Meat -- Limit Significantly (1-2x/month maximum)

- Lean cuts ONLY (tenderloin, sirloin, eye of round, 94% lean ground).
- Grass-fed preferred (better fat profile).
- Trim visible fat before cooking.
- 120-150g portions max.
- AVOID all processed meats: bacon, sausage, salami, prosciutto, hot dogs.

## Fats and Oils

### Use

- **Extra-virgin olive oil** -- #1 choice for heart health (room temp and low-heat).
- **Avocado oil** -- #1 choice for high-heat cooking.
- Cold-pressed, extra-virgin, organic preferred.

### Limit or Avoid

- **Coconut** (oil, milk, cream, shredded) -- 80-90% saturated fat, not ideal for cholesterol goals. Keep to occasional use.
- **Palm kernel/seed oil** -- same issue.
- **Refined, bleached, deodorized plant oils** -- avoid.
- **Butter** -- and foods containing butter (pastries, sauces).

### Practical Notes

- Coconut-based curries should be occasional, not regular rotation.
- When a recipe calls for coconut milk, consider cashew-based cream, or use coconut sparingly.

## Carbohydrates

### Emphasize (Whole, Fiber-Rich)

- Brown, red, purple/black/forbidden rice, wild rice.
- Quinoa, barley, farro, buckwheat, oats, amaranth, teff.
- Legumes (double duty as protein).
- Sweet potato, yam, pumpkin (with a touch of oil for nutrient absorption).
- 100% whole grain bread products (check: "whole" before each flour in ingredients, 2g+ fiber/serving).
- Oats: especially good for cholesterol (half cup dry = 1 serving). High in soluble fiber.

### Limit or Avoid

- White rice (including Basmati -- low fiber, not whole grain; brown Basmati is OK).
- White flour products: bread, pasta, noodles, pastries.
- Gluten-free does NOT equal whole grain. Most GF products use refined grains.
- Grain milks: rice milk, oat milk (avoid).

### Key Technique: Resistant Starch

Cook rice/potatoes, chill, then reheat. This converts some starch to resistant starch, lowering glycemic impact. Use this trick liberally for dinner carbs.

## Dairy

### Use (In Moderation)

- Greek yogurt: plain, unsweetened, low-fat (1-2%). Highest protein per serving.
- Unsweetened dairy milks.

### Limit

- Cheese: 25-30g max portions, just for flavor.
- Butter, cream, ice cream -- minimize.
- Full-fat dairy.

---

## Sweeteners and Treats

### Avoid or Minimize

- All granulated sugars (white, brown, raw, coconut, date).
- All syrups (honey, maple, agave, molasses, coconut nectar).
- Desserts, pastries, cookies, candy, regular chocolate.

### If Sweetening

Use whole pureed fruit (first choice), dried fruit or cold-pressed fruit juice (small amounts). Natural unrefined sweeteners (maple, coconut sugar) are marginally preferred but have identical glycemic impact -- use sparingly.

### Chocolate

Pure cacao or >80% dark chocolate, 30g portions, occasionally. HAS Chocolate 80% dark is the recommended brand (local Bali). Naturally dairy-free + high cacao is better than "vegan" chocolate that substitutes coconut fat.

## Vegetables and Fruits

### Vegetables -- Be Generous

- Fresh/raw in daytime, cooked in evening.
- Prioritize: dark leafy greens (kale, spinach, bok choy), cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts, cabbage), beets, carrots, pumpkin.
- Sweet potato and yam in smaller portions, served with oil.
- Moderate white potato (occasional, use resistant starch trick).

### Fruits

- Whole forms: fresh or frozen.
- Heart-beneficial: berries (all types), cooked tomato, avocado, olives.
- Limit concentrated forms: juices (120ml max), dried fruit (30-40g).

---

## Nuts and Seeds

- 30-40g daily as afternoon snack.
- Dry-roasted, toasted, or raw preferred (not oil-roasted).
- Star players: walnuts (30-40g/day shown to reduce cholesterol), Brazil nuts (1/day for selenium), almonds.
- Seeds: pumpkin, sunflower, sesame/tahini, chia (soaked), flax (ground, store in fridge).
- Nut butters: natural, no added oils.
- Unsweetened nut milks as dairy alternatives.

---

## Herbs and Spices

- Garlic and onion (highest antioxidants raw), turmeric, ginger, cinnamon.
- Fresh herbs: parsley, oregano, rosemary.
- Store in airtight containers (Ubud humidity degrades spices fast).

---

## Beverages

- Water: prioritize hydration (blood pressure support for Lydia).
- Coffee: 1/day, black or with unsweetened soy/cashew/almond milk.
- Avoid: sweetened drinks, fruit-only juices, ginger beer (sugar), rice/oat milk.
- Soda water + lime: fine as a default.

---

## Alcohol

Lydia does not drink. For Travis: moderate. The liver manages both alcohol and cholesterol -- regular alcohol makes it less efficient at cholesterol management.
